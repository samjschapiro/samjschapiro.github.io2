Philosophical Foundations of Intelligence

Exploring generalization and creativity from their very epistemological and metaphysical roots.

Our Goal

The goal of this reading group is to foster the philosophical discussions and inquiry needed to understand what form the next wave of scientific revolutions in AI will take.

Overview

This reading group is a continuation of the Philosophy and Psychology of Generalization and Creativity (PPGC) seminar and reading group at the Simons Institute, UC Berkeley as part of Modern Paradigms in Generalization (MPG) during Fall 2024

Generalization is roughly “the ability of an intelligent system to perform well on tasks outside of its training data.” The goal of PPGC was to understand how humans generalize, with particular emphasis on generalization during psychogenesis and extraordinary instances of generalization through scientific and industrial creativity. At the same time, we scrutinized landmark works in metaphysics and the philosophy of science to try to arrive at philosophical limits of generalization that can inform modern AI research.
